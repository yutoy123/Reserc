Three walls every LMIC researcher hits

Fieldnotes is built to bridge all three — not replace your thinking, but clear the path to your argument.

Data is scattered

World Bank, IMF, GSMA, UNDP, and national statistics offices use different formats, update cycles, and coverage gaps. Finding a number is easy; knowing which source to trust is not.

Frameworks are siloed

A student studying food insecurity in Ghana may not know ecological economics or behavioral nudge theory is directly relevant. Textbooks list fields equally — your topic does not.

Data → argument is hard

Knowing mobile money penetration is 60% in Kenya is easy. Knowing what that means for financial inclusion theory, and how to build a research argument around it, is the real work.
